Reflections from 12 STEM 3

Cachapero

Culture as with any other community is their identity. It is preserved through art, to be enjoyed not just by their very own people. But also by other people from other different cultures who wants to know and be educated on the art forms of other ethnicities. Through art it can be an integral bridge for people of different backgrounds to share and spread the knowledge that they know from their very own culture. This fosters and potentially builds a newer branch of art that will be enjoyed by future generations to come and for the cycle to be hopefully repeated because art at its core is not bound by the superficial restrictions of race or nationality.

Bongaros

Culture and arts play an important role in fostering connections among individuals from diverse backgrounds, as they serve as a bridge for people to communicate even with the barrier of language, as art is able to convey messages without the need for a specific language, but rather, it shows the emotions, history, and diversity of one’s culture. Through different forms of art, such as theater, painting, and music, we can further share our own personal experiences, the traditions that we are accustomed to, and our perspective that we can share. We can understand each other through art while promoting unity and respect. As we recognize the beauty of each art from different cultures and backgrounds, we are able to exchange information and appreciation towards each other despite us having different backgrounds or cultures. Ultimately, art can help us create a sense of belongingness and can strengthen bonds in different communities, allowing us to adapt and foster respect for each other.

Gaerlan

Understanding the diverse cultural backgrounds amongst other people leads to communication and acceptance. This is the very essence of connecting with other people. Discrimination, alienation, and racism won’t thrive through proper understanding. With the use of arts, this further leads to beautification and appreciation of other people’s cultures. This aids in strengthening bonds with other people, regardless of the historical backgrounds such as war and political issues behind the culture.

Maglutac

Culture and arts play a significant role in fostering connection among individuals from diverse backgrounds by encouraging understanding and appreciation of the different perspectives of one another. This shared experience acts as a bridge, helping people relate to one another and bringing them closer together. Through the power of culture and art, individuals come together as they exchange thoughts, fostering unity. Based on my experience, I have witnessed how art can create connection with people as we share different insights about the it. Some artworks, such as the Spoliarium, are popular, providing a common interest for discussion and deeper understanding. Moreover, engaging in small conversations and being able to share the knowledge we know enables both individuals to learn from one another. This exchange of ideas not only strengthens connections but also contributes to preserving history and cultural heritage.

Masiglat

Exposure to various forms of art helps individuals like us in appreciating diversity by looking at the world through various cultural lenses. For example, watching a traditional dance from another ethnicity or maybe listening to music from a different country can open up our minds to the experiences and stories we might never have encountered otherwise in our life. It also helps us understand that there are many ways on how we could express our emotions, ideas, and values in life. Art also has the power to challenge stereotypes by showcasing the depth and complexity of different cultures, proving to us that people from diverse backgrounds are more similar to each other than we might think. By engaging with these forms of art, we become more empathetic, learning how to be respectful and acknowledge our differences. Personally, after the Social Action Program with the Grade 8 Students, I realized how connected we all are, in terms of knowledge about our own history to the arts that we appreciate and relate to. With that, I can say that art has the power to unite people, creating bonds that we might not think to have existed otherwise.

Parreño

Engaging with diverse art forms is a transformative journey of personal growth and cross-cultural understanding, fostering appreciation for diversity and building bridges between cultures. Exposure to art from different cultures acts as a window into various worldviews, challenging preconceived notions and expanding our understanding of human experience. We gain insights into the values and beliefs reflected in the art, enriching our own perspectives and fostering empathy. The vibrant tapestry of diverse artistic expressions, whether through brushstrokes, notes, or words, reveals the boundless creativity and shared humanity of humankind. This shared appreciation for art's power to transcend boundaries creates a sense of community and cultivates meaningful connections, uniting us through our common appreciation for art's transformative power. The beauty and power of this diverse creativity reveal the boundless spirit of humankind, creating a foundation for meaningful connections with others who share an appreciation for art's ability to bridge cultural divides.

Ramos

Exposure to different forms of art helps people appreciate diversity by giving them a glimpse into different cultures and perspectives. It allows individuals to see how others express their stories, emotions, and beliefs. Art can break down barriers by showing that despite differences, everyone shares common feelings and experiences. It encourages empathy and understanding, making people more open-minded. Whether through music, paintings, or performances, art creates connections between individuals. These shared experiences bring people closer together and promote respect for different backgrounds. Ultimately, art helps build a more inclusive and united community.

Ticman

Exposure to different forms of art helps individuals appreciate diversity by offering insights into various cultural expressions, values, and traditions. Whether through music, visual arts, theater, or literature, art invites people to experience the world through the lens of others, fostering empathy and broadening perspectives. This experience encourages individuals to recognize common human emotions and experiences despite cultural differences. By engaging with art from diverse backgrounds, people also learn to value the uniqueness of different cultures while celebrating shared themes of love, struggle, joy, and resilience. Art thus becomes a bridge for meaningful connections, creating opportunities for dialogue and understanding. Ultimately, exposure to diverse art forms enhances mutual respect and strengthens the bonds between individuals from varied cultural backgrounds.
