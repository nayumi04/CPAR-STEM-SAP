THE HISTORY BEHIND THE NATIONAL MUSEUM OF FINE ARTS

The National Museum of Fine Arts is a large and historic building in Manila that displays many important artworks from the Philippines. It was originally the Legislative Building, where government meetings were held, but it was later turned into a museum to showcase the country’s rich art and culture.On February 19, 2025, the STEM Strand of San Beda University visited the museum. We had the chance to witness the Spoliarium in person and learn about its background, including its meaning and historical importance. Inside, visitors can explore different galleries filled with beautiful paintings, sculptures, and old drawings. Some artworks show scenes from Philippine history, while others capture everyday life, nature, and traditional customs. The museum’s design is also grand, with tall columns, high ceilings, and wide halls that give it a majestic feel. The museum is part of the National Museum of the Philippines and is free to visit, making it a great place for students, tourists, and art lovers to learn about the country’s culture and history.
