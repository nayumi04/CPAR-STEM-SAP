The Old Senate Session Hall

The Old Senate Session Hall features the Filipino Struggles Through History, which is also known as the history of manila. These paintings were painted by Carlos “Botong” V. Francisco, who was commissioned in 1968 for the Manila City Hall by Mayor Antonio J. Villegas. The Old Senate Session Hall is a historic chamber which was once used as a meeting place for the senate from 1926 up until 1996. It was designed to be a “secular cathedral”, which symbolizes a temple of wisdom to open for debate and lawmaking. Throughout its own history, the hall had significant legislative debates, this also included discussions from the country’s challenge for full independence from the U.S in the 1930s. After the senate had relocated in 1996, the building went into restoration and is now part of the National Museum. It is now repurposed as an exhibition space, specifically housing the piece named “Filipino Struggles Through History”.
