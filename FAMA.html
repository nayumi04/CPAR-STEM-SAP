Spoliarium by Juan Luna

The museum’s most famous artwork is Juan Luna’s “Spoliarium,” a massive painting that shows fallen gladiators being dragged away. This painting is important because it symbolizes the suffering of Filipinos during the Spanish colonization. The museum also features works by Fernando Amorsolo, Félix Resurrección Hidalgo, and Guillermo Tolentino, as well as other talented Filipino artists.
