Faith, Tradition, and Place Exhibit

The Faith, Tradition, and Place Exhibit: Bangsamoro Art from the National Ethnographic Collection opened way back October 2014 together with Magbassa Kita Foundation Inc. in accordance with the celebration of Eid’l Adha. This part of the museum highlights the rich material cultural heritage of the Islamic cultures in Mindanao, mostly the Maranaos. The artworks presented emphasise that these objects are essential as a part of feasts, and to the Bangsamoro culture while also tackling political issues, social identities, memories, development, and more. These artifacts include intricately carved wooden panels, brassware, musical instruments, textiles, and weaponry, all showcasing the craftsmanship and artistry of the Bangsamoro people. One of the key themes of the exhibit is the strong Islamic influence on Bangsamoro art, seen in geometric and floral patterns that avoid human or animal representations, in line with Islamic artistic traditions. The exhibit also explores the role of art in daily life, from decorating homes and mosques to adornments for boats and grave markers. The artworks not only enriches the culture of the Maranaos but as well as their history in which they have also showcased some armors used by the ancient Maranao warriors and their gears.
