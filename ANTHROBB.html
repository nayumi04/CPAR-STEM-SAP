Baybayin Gallery

Some of us may not be aware that there is an ancient form of writing that existed in the country even before the arrival of the spaniards. Baybayin Gallery contains evidence such as archeological artifacts and old written documents to showcase the rich and beautiful form of writing called baybayin such as the Monreal stones, Laguna Copperplate inscription and a facsimile of Doctrina Christiana, the first book printed in the country. Moreover, the gallery also highlights the writing of the indigenous people of Mindoro and Palawan which is the ambahan and the tagbanwa. A section also features the revitalization of Baybayin script interest among the present generations of Filipinos, particularly the youth, in the Philippines and all over the world.
