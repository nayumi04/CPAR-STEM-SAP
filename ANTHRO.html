THE HISTORY BEHIND THE NATIONAL MUSEUM OF ANTHROPOLOGY

The National Museum of Anthropology in Manila is one of the main components of the National Museum of the Philippines. It was built between 1916 and 1918. The museum focuses on ethnological and archaeological galleries. The Philippines' rich cultural heritage and archaeological history was showcased through the fascinating collections of artifacts. The museum is located in Rizal Park, within a neoclassical building designed by Ralph Harrington Doane. The museum is now considered to be a narration of the Philippinesâ€™ past that is preserved and shared through a collection of arts and artifacts, including the building itself. Its building is a reconstruction of the Department of Finance and Legislative buildings of the Japanese, which was almost completely destroyed by the American forces during the last days of the World War II's Battle in Manila.
