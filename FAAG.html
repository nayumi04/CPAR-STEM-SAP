The Assassination of Governor Bustamante by Felix Resurreccion Hidalgo

The Assassination of Governor Bustamante,also known as Church Versus State (El Asesinato del Gobernador Bustamante/La Iglesia contra el Estado), is an oil painting made by Resurreccion Hidalgo in 1904. It depicts the assassination of Governor-General Fernando Bustamante y Bustillo in 1719 by the hands of friars who stormed the Palacio del Gobernador in response to Bustamante imprisoning the Archbishop and disregarding the church. The painting features strong diagonal lines along the spears and lances which suggests movements. The painting also features bright colors along the governor's robes, and also uses a play on dark and bright lighting to further intensify the emotions within the painting. There is also a a sequel to this event which also has a painting, called The Black She-Wolf (La Loba Negra). The Assassination of Governor Bustamante was commissioned by Don Antonio Ma. Regidor, he however was not able to claim the painting. The painting stayed with Hidalgo in Barcelona until a year after Hidalgo's death when hi relatives were able to take the painting back to the Philippines. The painting was declared a National Cultural Treasure and first shown in 1974 at the National Museum of the Philippines, but was not to be kept in the museum until October 29, 2013 when it was formally turned over.
