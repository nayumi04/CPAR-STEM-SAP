Filipino Struggles Through History by Carlos “Botong” Francisco

“Filipino Struggles Through History” is a mural by renowned Filipino muralist and National Artist for painting Carlos “Botong” Francisco. The artwork employs modernist techniques to vividly portray 600 years of the Philippines' history through the use of dynamic forms, fluid movement, and bold colors. The mural was commissioned by the mayor of Manila, Antonio Villegas, in 1964. Named “Kasaysayan ng Maynila” (translated to “History of Manila”), the artwork consists of seven oil on canvas panels each displaying numerous events ranging from the pre-colonial era up to the nation’s fierce battle against colonization and oppression. Francisco intricately blends the different historical moments illustrating the ongoing longing for freedom. The mural was hung at the Bulwagang Katipunan in Manila City Hall and in 1996 it was declared a National Cultural Treasure. Francisco beautifully captures the spirit of the Filipino people and stands as a form of self expression, art, and a reminder of the brutal sacrifices endured for freedom.
